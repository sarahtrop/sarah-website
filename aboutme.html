What I've Been Up to Lately

Grinnell AppDev

The Scarlet & Black

I have also been working as a freelance photographer for my college’s newspaper, The Scarlet & Black, on a regular basis for the past one and a half years. Smithsonian Arctic Studies Center

In 2013, I was an intern in the Arctic Studies Center working under Dr. Bill Fitzhugh. My project consisted of using Adobe Photoshop to edit photographs taken by the naturalist Edward Nelson during an Alaskan expedition in the late 1800s (the photographs were originally taken on glass plates) to enhance the digital quality and make the subject matter more easily discernible. After editing the photographs, I paired them with Nelson’s diaries using the cross-referencing found in the diaries and mentions in the book that Nelson wrote when he returned from Alaska. I then catalogued the photographs and their references in the diaries and Nelson’s book in a short database for future use by the Smithsonian. This project will eventually be a part of the ASC’s website on Edward Nelson. While at the ASC, I also digitized several thousand slides from various exhibits and research expeditions sponsored by the ASC.
