work

Industrial Light & Magic

This summer, I'm interning in Core Pipeline at ILM in San Francisco. I'm spearheading a project from start to finish, rebuilding an existing application (which has a mostly manual workflow) in an automated Django-based web application. I'm learning a lot about Django, Python, and working with different engineering teams across the pipeline. It's an incredible experience, and I absolutely love it.

Grinnell Appdev

At Grinnell, I was the Director of Design for two years on the application development team, Grinnell Appdev. Appdev is a team of select Grinnell College students who work to design and develop mobile applications usable within the Grinnell community. I have worked on the redesign of current apps, as well as website pages, and training new designers in both UX and UI. I was a UI Designer and the webmaster for a semester, and was then promoted to Design Lead, as well as webmaster.

Grinnell Contra Dance

My second year at Grinnell, I started the Grinnell Contra Dance Club. At first it was just me and I ran a couple of workshops and one full two and a half hour dance that spring. In my last two years at Grinnell I grew the contra club at Grinnell to weekly workshops and three dances a year, with about thirty regulars each week. I'm so incredibly proud of the fun and inclusive community we built around dancing, and the skills the students have learned in the past year or so. I had so much fun doing it, and I feel incredibly fortunate to be able to share it with them. When I graduated I passed the baton to a few second and first years, and I'm confident they will do a great job. If you want to see what contra dancing looks like, take a peek.

CS Mentoring

For three semesters, I was a mentor for the introductory Computer Science Course at Grinnell, CSC 151 - Functional Problem Solving. This class is conducted in the Scheme langauge using DrRacket. I took this class previously as a part of my major and enjoyed it immensely, so when I was asked to be a mentor, I jumped at the opportunity. Mentoring is one of the highlights of my week, and the second semester was just as fun and rewarding as the first. This past fall I also mentored a new course, entitled "The Art of Code". The class was entirely online, and was a great learning experience for me as much as for the students. For my last semester at Grinnell, I'm mentored CSC 207 - Data Structures & Object Oriented Programming, the Java and object-oriented course at Grinnell.
