Scarlet & Black }Facebook Twitter Website{

I have also been working as a freelance photographer for my college’s newspaper, The Scarlet & Black, on a regular basis for the past one and a half years. My assignment changes on a weekly basis and ranges from portraits, sports, to campus events. Last semester, I participated in a new program within the newspaper in which first-year photographers shadowed upperclassmen to learn the full scope of the newspaper photography process – from picking assignments to taking the photos, editing in Adobe Photoshop and finally submitting to the paper.

ALSO Italian }Grinnell Twitter Website{

I am an Italian tutor in the Alternative Language Study Option at Grinnell, a program that provdes individual driven learning for languages not taught at the college with peer tutors whom are fluent in the chosen language. This semester I am tutoring Italian II one other tutor, with whom I work closesly to structure the curriculum and the activities we construct for interactive learning during our biweekly tutoring sessions with the students. I am also crafting a new online curriculum using the Haiku Learning platform to supplement the in class activities.

Artic Studies Center } Twitter Website {

In 2013, I was an intern in the Arctic Studies Center working under Dr. Bill Fitzhugh. My project consisted of using Adobe Photoshop to edit photographs taken by the naturalist Edward Nelson during an Alaskan expedition in the late 1800s (the photographs were originally taken on glass plates) to enhance the digital quality and make the subject matter more easily discernible. I then catalogued the photographs and their references in the diaries and Nelson’s book in a short database for future use by the Smithsonian. This project will eventually be a part of the ASC’s website on Edward Nelson. While at the ASC, I also digitized several thousand slides from various exhibits and research expeditions sponsored by the ASC.
