Grinnell AppDev

I am currently the Design Lead on the Application Development team, Grinnell AppDev. AppDev is a team of select Grinnell College students who work to design and develop mobile applications usable within the Grinnell community. I have worked on the redesign of current apps, as well as website pages. I was a UI Designer and the webmaster for a semester, and have just been promoted to Design Lead, as well as webmaster. To see examples of my work at AppDev, check out my portfolio!

Uffizi Gallery

This semester while I'm in Florence studying art history and Italian, I'm also interning at the Uffizi Gallery. I'm very excited to get started working at the gallery, working on my Italian and helping out with new and current exhibits.
SCLDA

This past summer I was fortunate enough to to intern with the Smithsonian Center for Learning and Digital Access. My main project while at SCLDA was to build digital content for the new online learning platform, called the Learning Lab, launching this fall. I also worked on reconstructing existing resources, like lesson plans and the online heritage tours, into the new platform, and presented to units across the institution about the Learning Lab.
CS Mentoring

This spring I was a mentor for the introductory Computer Science Course at Grinnell, CSC 151 - Functional Problem Solving. This class is conducted in the Scheme langauge using DrRacket. I took this class previously as a part of my major and enjoyed it immensely, so when I was asked to be a mentor, I jumped at the opportunity. ALSO Italian

I am an Italian tutor in the Alternative Language Study Option at Grinnell, a program that provdes individual driven learning with peer tutors whom are fluent in the chosen language. In the spring, I taught Italian II and restructured the curriculum and classroom activities on our new platform, Haiku Learning.
Ignite Teacher Scholar

This past semester I was a part of the Ignite Program at Grinnell, a program which enrolls local students in mini-classes taught and created by college students. This spring the theme was "Culture, Language, and Difference," and my fellow Italian tutor Mark and I wrote a class about Rome. Our class incorportated Italian vocabulary and history of Rome as well as engaging the 5th and 6th grade students in activities based around gladiator culture.
