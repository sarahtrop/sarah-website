work

Grinnell AppDev

I am currently the Director of Design on the Application Development team at my school, Grinnell AppDev. AppDev is a team of select Grinnell College students who work to design and develop mobile applications usable within the Grinnell community. I have worked on the redesign of current apps, as well as website pages, and training new designers in both UX and UI. I was a UI Designer and the webmaster for a semester, and was then promoted to Design Lead, as well as webmaster.

CS Mentoring

For three semesters, I have been a mentor for the introductory Computer Science Course at Grinnell, CSC 151 - Functional Problem Solving. This class is conducted in the Scheme langauge using DrRacket. I took this class previously as a part of my major and enjoyed it immensely, so when I was asked to be a mentor, I jumped at the opportunity. Mentoring is one of the highlights of my week, and the second semester was just as fun and rewarding as the first. This past fall I also mentored a new course, entitled "The Art of Code". The class was entirely online, and was a great learning experience for me as much as for the students.
Grinnell Contra Dance

My second year at Grinnell, I started the Grinnell Contra Dance Club. At first it was just me, and occasionally a friend of mine helping out, and I ran a couple of workshops that each had about eight people in attendance and one two and a half hour dance that spring. Since then, I have grown the contra club at Grinnell to weekly workshops and three dances a year, with about thirty regulars each week. I now work with my friend Julia Hart in running the club, and we are in the process of teaching new students how to continue it after we graduate. I'm so incredibly proud of the fun and inclusive community we've built around dancing, and the skills the students have learned in the past year or so. I have so much fun doing it, and I feel incredibly fortunate to be able to share it with them. If you want to see what contra dancing looks like, take a peek.
